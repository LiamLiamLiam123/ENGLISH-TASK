A life rooted in a small country town,
shaped by family, cricket, and 23 years of building something his own.

⚠️ Disclaimer: The story mentions death and surgery

I was born on 13 March 1974, on a Wednesday. That day, my mum and dad were amazed because I was the 2nd baby they successfully had after trying 3 other times. I have one older brother named "Greg". He is the reason why I am the way I am today with Holden's and music. When I was older, I remember sitting with my nana by the trees, telling her about who my kindergarten teacher was. I was also moving bricks one by one about ten metres to help my dad build what actually turned out to be an illegal sunroom. I grew up in Bundanoon with a fairly simple childhood in a country town with a small primary school, the kind where everyone knew each other. Life moved the whole time quietly. I was mainly interested in bikes growing up and also bike riding, cricket, soccer, hockey, depending on the season. I also bred budgies. Primary school was small and happy. I was happy when I was planting a gum tree that is still there to this day. The last time I saw it, it had grown really big. Then Moss Vale High School through to Year 12. I don't really remember anything bad that happened, other than when I failed my HSCs. When I was in uni, I realised I had lots of fun, for the most part. The biggest Influence was my brother Greg, he taught me what songs were and some actual good songs. Unlike today, for sports and all that stuff, it was my grandfather, he taught me some old games in his backyard. My favourite sport was squash when I was younger and still is. For the work ethic, it was definitely my father he taught me how to maintain a business. I believe strongly in treat other the way you want to be treated, basically just respect. The biggest challenge I have had to face was when my father passed away at the age of 64, it was unexpected. Although he did have some minor health issues, we didn't think it was that bad. I became more emotionally weak, but I have recovered over time. That moment has changed me as a person. I had time to heal, it took a while, but I got used to it, and I still cry on his birthday and the day he passed. I am most proud of the time I built a business for myself in 23 years from the ground up from scratch. In the future, I plan to retire, but make sure my kids are set up for success and for life as much as I can do. If I could reverse time, I would tell my younger self, "Don't be so hard on yourself, and travel if you can". I don't really know what I want to be remembered for or by, i just hope my kids will never forget me or make me sad. The five earliest memories of my childhood would be the bricks and telling Nana about K teacher, everyone in my class in K getting chicken pox (including the teacher) other than me, learning to ride my bike, and being the first passenger in brother's first car. Other milestones I have done are being the first member of either side of the family to go to uni. Been owner of my own business for 23 years. Three things I want to tell my younger self are to travel more, have more confidence, and have more fun in life. I met a woman named Joanne Owen Thomas over the phone, and she flies to Australia from New Zealand, and we have a work meeting. We started dating after we realised we actually liked each other. We hit it off well and married each other on the 23rd of December 2006. We had a honeymoon, we bought a house together, and then in 2011 we had a kid on the 22nd of November 2011 named Liam John Stephenson, my mum was there, and so was Greg. Greg even gave Liam a Teddy bear bigger than him, and he still has it to this very day. My mum was saying how strong and handsome and cool Liam was and how he would become very successful in life. 3 years later, we had Harper Mechana Stephenson, she, unlike Liam, did not receive a very big teddy. Instead, Liam gave her a nickname that would stick forever. When Liam said, "Can I hold IT?" We burst out laughing. Years later, Liam and I had gone to Victoria for the first time for the Great Vic Bike ride. We had to bail halfway through because Liam got scared of all the big hills, he had bought his laptop with him so I could play Minecraft with him. Years later, we have Liam in Year 9, Harper in Year 6, and I am doing the best I can. Though a couple of months ago I had to have emergency surgery because there was an infection in my left pinkie toe, my family was telling me to go to the hospital, but I didn't listen because my type of personality is to push things aside for everyone else even when I am sick, although Liam doesn't take advantage of that. I had a machine in my arm that cleans the pus from the cut off toe. Today I am well, No moon boot, No vacume, No injurys The End
