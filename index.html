Places in his story

Where did you grow up, and what was your childhood like?

Bundanoon - a fairly simple childhood in a small country town with a small primary school. Life moved quietly and everyone knew everyone.

What are some of your earliest memories?

Sitting with my Nana, telling her about who my Kindergarten teacher would be. And moving bricks one by one - about 10 metres - to help Dad build what turned out to be an illegal sunroom.

A small town where everyone knew everyone.

- On Bundanoon

What hobbies or interests did you have growing up?

Bike riding, cricket, soccer or hockey depending on the season - and breeding budgies.

What was your school life like?

Primary school was small and happy - I even planted a gum tree that's still there today, very big. Then Moss Vale High School through to Year 12. I really don't have too many bad memories from that time.

Primary school

Uni. Looking back now I can see it was fun for the most part.

Who has had the biggest influence on your life?

For music, my brother Greg. For sport and cricket in particular, my Grandfather. For Holdens, also my brother. For work ethic, my Dad.

What values are most important to you?

Respect - treat others how you expect to be treated.

What is a big challenge you've faced in your life?

My Dad passing away. It was unexpected - he was only 64.

I became significantly more emotional. That moment changed me as a person.

- On losing his father

How did you overcome that challenge?

Time to heal. Trying not to think about it too much - but remembering when appropriate.

What achievement are you most proud of?

Twenty-three years building my own business from the ground up.

What goals do you have for the future?

Retire from business - but making sure the kids are set up as much as possible first.

What advice would you give to your younger self?

Don't be so hard on myself. And actually - do travel.

Don't be so hard on yourself. Actually do travel.

What do you want your legacy or story to be?

I really don't know. I guess just to be remembered by those who are close to me.
